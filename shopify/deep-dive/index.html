About Me

How did you get into design?

I started coding in high school with Visual Basic 6. The drag-and-drop GUI was my first exposure to code and design, and when I eventually learned more coding languages, I spent more and more time designing. It was something that was more interesting and had a more human element.

It was easy to update and reiterate on changes with interactive prototypes, and that brought more satisfaction. Seeing users going from frustration to happiness is what drives me.

What does design mean to you?

Design means anticipating user needs. It is knowing what to present to the user, when, and how. Fail fast and fail often is a phrase I believe to be important and true.

How do you stay current on design patterns?

I read several curated news feeds like Sidebar.io and NNGroup, as well as posts from authors like Julie Zhuo. I draw inspiration from my hobbies as well, which include video games, cooking, climbing, and movies.

What tools do you use?

I like to use Adobe CC for mockups due to its flexibility and compatibility between other apps in the suite (like Ae, Ps, Ai). I prefer to use HTML/CSS for prototypes due to its speed, accuracy, animation, and its ability to replicate real results.
