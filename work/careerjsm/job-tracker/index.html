A Better Job Tracker

Our original Job Tracker tool was displayed in a table format (similar to the one pictured above), and users were having trouble using it efficiently. Engagement was low, the navigation was tedious, and the information display was very dense.

Having used other project management tools, the product/development team already had a few ideas for how to tackle this issue. We decided take a week to do a Design Sprint to refine and test our new ideas.

The Design Sprint outline, from Jake Knapp's Design Sprint.

I facilitated the week-long design sprint with the team to search for better solutions. After much discussion, sketches, and deliberation over 2.5 days, we ended up with a rough storyboard outline for how new feature would work, look, and feel.

The storyboard created by on 3.

Under my guidance The team spent then next day and a half collaboratively putting ideas and designs together in Figma. I then refined and organized the pieces together to form a cohesive interactive prototype, roughly following the storyboard. After this, I designed the user-testing script and questionnaire, double checking with the prototype to ensure tests would run smoothly.

Some design components in Figma.

On the last day, I conducted guerilla user testing, and analyzed the results to present to the team by the end of the day. It yielded overall positive results, as well as useful feedback. I drafted up some product requirement documents and added tasks to our project tracker tool, addressing weaknesses and inconsistencies. Over the next couple of weeks, the team would build out the full-featured product and make the appropriate adjustments.
