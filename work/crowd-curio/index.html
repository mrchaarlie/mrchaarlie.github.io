What
UX Design

A research platform

CrowdCurio is a crowd sourced platform that allows everyday people or "citizen scientists" to contribute to scientific research. Similar to sites like Galaxy Zoo, it relies on users to help sort and catalog data that would be otherwise very difficult or impossible to do (at this point in time) with machines. As a platform, it was meant for anyone and everyone to be able to share, conduct, and contribute to experiments across the world. Being the first designer and front-end developer, I helped create the initial layout and configuration of web app.

Wireframe designs.

From idea to product

Crowd Curio initially had several requirements:

  • allow for the uploading & management of experiments by "scientist" users
  • create custom modules/interfaces for the different types of research topic
  • gamify the experiments to increase engagement
  • make new experiments easily discoverable
In order to satisfy these needs, I started off by creating several paper wireframes and a site map. After design iterations, I converted them to an HTML prototype.

Initial prototype screenshots, 2015.

Project Focus

The project had a fairly large scope, so a team of 2 people couldn't easily realize all the requirements in a short amount of time. I directed design and development towards one specific pilot project, with an interface optimized for that singular experiment.

After conducting user testing to determine the best layout, where user interests were, and what gamification elements worked best, I made adjustments accordingly.

The finalized v1 design implementation, 2015.
